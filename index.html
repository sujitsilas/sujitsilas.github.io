About Me

I'm a second-year MS student in Bioengineering at UCLA, with a focus biomedical data sciences. My research interests span single-cell genomics, deep learning applications in biology, and cancer genomics. I’m currently working with data related to differential methylation and methylation patterns in cancer at GRAIL. Before UCLA, I earned a BS in Biology from UC Riverside, where I worked on various bioinformatics algorithms and analysis tools for immunology. I’m passionate about using computational methods to unravel complex biological questions and aim to apply these skills to improve healthcare outcomes.
