About Me

Hello! I'm Sujit Silas, a computational biologist with close to five years of experience in the field. I have a robust research track record and exceptional analytical skills, which have helped contribute to various projects at the intersection of biology and informatics.

I am currently pursuing a Master's degree in Bioengineering at the Henry Samueli School of Engineering, UCLA. As part of my academic journey, I have been serving as a graduate student researcher at the Allard Laboratory, where I explore transgenerational toxicity.

Over the summer of 2024, I had the opportunity to intern at GRAIL as a computational biology and machine learning scientist, working on investigating false positives and developing advanced visualization tools.

Before my master's, I was a bioinformatics specialist at La Jolla Institute for Immunology, where I drove bioinformatics initiatives, analyzing diverse next-generation sequencing datasets to identify genetic markers in inflammatory diseases under the guidance of Dr. Klaus Ley, MD. I innovated, standardized, and optimized computational algorithms, contributing to 10 high-impact publications.

I am committed to utilizing my expertise to drive meaningful advancements in the life sciences. I welcome collaborations and opportunities to delve further into this captivating realm. Let's connect and explore the endless possibilities together!
