About Me

I'm a graduate student researcher at UCLA's Henry Samueli School of Engineering, pursuing my Master's in Bioengineering. At the Allard Laboratory, I focus on exploring transgenerational toxicity, combining computational and experimental approaches to understand complex biological systems.

Previously, I worked as a bioinformatics scientist at La Jolla Institute for Immunology, where I led various bioinformatics initiatives analyzing next-generation sequencing datasets to identify genetic markers in inflammatory diseases. My work has contributed to 10 high-impact publications in the field.

Research Interests

  • Computational Biology: single cell genomics, structural biology, cancer genomics
  • Machine Learning: deep learning, bioinformatics algorithms, data analysis
  • Genomics: next-generation sequencing, inflammatory diseases, genetic markers
